Morning Serenity: CITCLS Leads the Rosary

  • @UASSITE
  • May 15, 2024
  • Comments

The College of Information Technology Computing and Library Science began this early morning with a serene and reflective moment, leading the Rosary. Faculty, students, and staff gathered together, united in prayer and contemplation, to start the day with spiritual grounding and a sense of peace. The soft murmur of prayers created a calm and tranquil atmosphere, setting a positive tone for the day's activities. This moment of collective devotion not only strengthened individual faith but also fostered a deeper sense of community and shared purpose within the college. Through this spiritual practice, participants found a renewed focus and inspiration to carry through their academic and personal endeavors.

"Be still, and know that I am God; I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth." — Psalm 46:10 (NIV)

This verse emphasizes the importance of taking moments of stillness and reflection, aligning perfectly with the serene start to the day with the Rosary.

A Serene Start: Leading the Rosary in the Morning

As the day progressed, the positive impact of the morning’s Rosary continued to resonate. Conversations during breaks were filled with warmth and empathy, reflecting the deeper connections formed through shared spiritual practice. The college environment, often bustling with the excitement and pressures of academic life, felt harmoniously aligned with a sense of tranquility and mutual respect. This experience underscored the importance of integrating moments of reflection and spiritual growth into the busy rhythms of academic life, fostering not only intellectual development but also emotional and spiritual well-being.
