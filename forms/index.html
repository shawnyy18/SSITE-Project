Welcome to SSITE Organization

Student Society on Information Technology Education (SSITE) is an organization made for CITCLS Students. It aims to promote and develop future graduates the skills and values needed for their future. The organization aims to support and destress students with the programs and activities.

Read More

Mission

The mission of SSITE is to offer comprehensive assistance to students, addressing their academic and personal needs. We strive to provide engaging activities and foster active connections among students, ensuring they have access to the resources and support necessary for both their educational and recreational pursuits.

Read More

Objectives

Offering support to CITCLS students, helping them to manage their stress and encouraging them to contribute positively to society. By providing a supportive environment, SSITE aim to alleviate their stress and motivate them to engage in community service, fostering a sense of purpose and connection.

Coding Bootcamp Series

A series of intensive coding bootcamps aimed at enhancing students' programming skills. Each bootcamp focuses on a different programming language or technology, such as Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, and SQL. The sessions include hands-on workshops, coding challenges, and collaborative projects.

Tech Talk Tuesdays

Weekly sessions where industry professionals and alumni share insights on the latest trends and developments in the IT field. Topics could include cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, blockchain, and software development best practices. The format includes presentations, Q&A sessions, and networking opportunities.

Hackathon Weekend

A 48-hour hackathon event where students form teams to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems. Participants brainstorm ideas, build prototypes, and present their projects to a panel of judges. Prizes are awarded for the most creative and impactful solutions.

Community Tech Outreach

A program that encourages students to use their IT skills for community service. Activities could include teaching basic computer skills to local residents, developing websites for non-profit organizations, or organizing tech drives to collect and refurbish old computers for donation.

The UA-CITCLS extension program from 2021 to present with the Dominican School of Angeles City, Mining, Angeles City.

The key points of the discussion included the provision of comprehensive Google Workspace for Education training for the school's faculty and staff, aimed at enhancing their digital skills and educational tools.
